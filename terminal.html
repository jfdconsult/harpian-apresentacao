Behavioral Context Layer
Why Does It Matter?

Clients rarely experience capital as one single portfolio. In practice, they organize money into mental compartments: safety, lifestyle, retirement, future family needs, legacy, real estate, liquidity, travel, business reserves and long-term growth.

These mental compartments often exist below the surface. The client may feel them clearly, but they are not always formalized in the portfolio, in the mandate, or in the day-to-day communication between client and manager.

The purpose of this layer is to make those implicit objectives explicit, creating a mutual alignment pact between client and manager around:

  • investment horizon and expected use of capital;
  • return expectations and the Risk Number needed to pursue them;
  • behavior during volatility, drawdowns and market stress;
  • how the advisor should communicate market events to that specific client;
  • which portfolio risks are acceptable, confusing or emotionally difficult.

When this context is captured, JIM can interpret the portfolio with more precision. A position is no longer evaluated only by asset class or Risk Number; it is also interpreted against the purpose, timing and behavioral expectation attached to that capital.

This improves the relationship because the client understands why the portfolio behaves the way it does, the advisor has a clearer language for difficult conversations, and the investment journey becomes calmer, more transparent and better aligned.

This layer does not change the portfolio and does not generate recommendations. It improves the quality of interpretation, communication and alignment.
